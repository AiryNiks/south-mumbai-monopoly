# South Mumbai Monopoly - Complete Rules

## Objective
Become the wealthiest player by buying, renting, and trading properties while forcing other players into bankruptcy.

## Game Setup

### Starting Money
Each player starts with 1,500 Mumbai Rupees (MR):
- 2 × 500 MR notes
- 2 × 100 MR notes
- 2 × 50 MR notes
- 5 × 20 MR notes
- 5 × 10 MR notes
- 5 × 5 MR notes
- 5 × 1 MR notes

### Initial Placement
1. Place one token per player on "Go"
2. Choose a Banker (usually the player who played South Mumbai locations)
3. Banker shuffles Chance and Community Chest cards separately
4. Determine play order by highest dice roll

## Turn Structure

### During Your Turn
1. **Roll the Dice**: Roll two dice and move your token clockwise
   - Move the total number of spaces shown
   - If you roll doubles, roll again (but see "Go to Jail" rules)
   - Landing on a space takes the action described below

2. **Take the Action** for the space you landed on (see Space Actions below)

3. **Buy Property** (optional): If you land on an unowned property, you may buy it
   - Offer a purchase price to the Banker (Banker's asking price applies)
   - If you don't buy, Banker auctions it to any player (highest bid wins)

4. **End Your Turn**: Pass the dice to the next player

### Special: Rolling Doubles
- If both dice show the same number, you move again
- Exception: If you roll doubles and land on "Go to Jail," you go to jail (see below)
- If you roll doubles three times in one turn, go directly to Jail

## Space Actions

### Corner Spaces (4)

**Go (Position 0)**
- Start position
- Collect 200 MR when landing here
- Collect 200 MR when passing through
- No other action

**Go to Jail (Positions 10 & 30)**
- Move directly to Jail
- Pay no fine
- Your turn ends

**Free Parking (Position 20)**
- Rest free of charge
- No penalties or rewards
- Your turn continues

**Income Tax (Position 4)**
- Pay either:
  - 200 MR flat, OR
  - 10% of your total assets (buildings, properties, cash)
  - Choose the lower amount

**Luxury Tax (Position 38)**
- Pay 100 MR to the Banker

### Property Spaces

#### Buying Properties
1. If you land on an unowned property, you may buy it for the price shown
2. If you don't buy, it goes to auction (any player can bid)
3. Once you own a property, place your Title Deed card in front of you

#### Rent Payment
1. If you land on a property owned by another player, pay rent to the owner
2. Rent depends on what buildings are on the property:
   - **Unimproved**: Base rent (shown on card)
   - **1-4 Houses**: Rent increases per house
   - **1 Hotel**: Hotel rent (replaces houses)

#### Monopoly Bonus
When you own all properties of one color:
- Rent on unimproved properties doubles
- You can build houses and hotels on this color group

#### Building Houses
1. All houses must be built on all properties of a monopoly
2. You must build evenly (no more than 1 house difference between properties)
3. Cost per house: shown on property card (50-200 MR depending on color)
4. You can have 4 houses per property
5. You can replace 4 houses with 1 hotel

#### Building Hotels
1. Requires 4 houses on a property
2. Return 4 houses to the Banker
3. Hotel rent is highest rent for that property
4. Cost: shown on property card (200-500 MR depending on color)

#### Mortgaging Properties
1. You can mortgage an unimproved property to the Banker
2. Receive half the property value as cash
3. Other players cannot collect rent on mortgaged properties
4. To un-mortgage: pay back the mortgage amount plus 10% interest
5. You cannot build on mortgaged properties

### Railway Stations (4)
- **Central Railway Station** (Victoria Terminus - Position 5)
- **Fort Railway Station** (Position 15)
- **Churchgate Railway Station** (Position 25)
- **Grant Road Railway Station** (Position 35)

Rent depends on how many you own:
- 1 railway: 25 MR
- 2 railways: 50 MR
- 3 railways: 100 MR
- 4 railways: 200 MR

### Utilities (2)
- **Fort Port Authority** (Electricity - Position 12)
- **Grant Road Municipality** (Water - Position 28)

Rent depends on how many you own:
- 1 utility: 4 × dice roll
- 2 utilities: 10 × dice roll

### Chance Cards (16 Cards)
When you land on a Chance space:
1. Take the top card from the Chance pile
2. Follow the card's instructions
3. Return the card to the bottom of the pile

#### Chance Card Actions
- Advance to Go (collect 200 MR)
- Advance to specific properties
- Advance to a railway (collect next railway income if you own it)
- Go back three spaces
- Go directly to Jail
- Pay street repairs (25 MR per house, 100 MR per hotel)
- Pay fines or collect rewards
- Get Out of Jail Free card

### Community Chest Cards (16 Cards)
When you land on a Community Chest space:
1. Take the top card from the Community Chest pile
2. Follow the card's instructions
3. Return the card to the bottom of the pile

#### Community Chest Actions
- Advance to Go (collect 200 MR)
- Go directly to Jail
- Collect various amounts
- Pay various amounts
- Get Out of Jail Free card

## Jail Rules

### Going to Jail
You go to Jail when you:
- Land on "Go to Jail" space
- Draw a "Go to Jail" card
- Roll doubles three times in one turn

### In Jail
1. You are "Just Visiting" on your first turn in jail
2. You can still collect rent on your properties
3. You can still buy properties or build
4. You cannot move; your turn ends

### Getting Out of Jail
On your turn while in jail, you must:
- **Pay 50 MR bail and roll the dice**, OR
- **Roll doubles** (if you do, move normally and continue playing), OR
- **Use a Get Out of Jail Free card**

If you roll doubles, you move and take your turn normally.
If you don't roll doubles after 3 turns, pay 50 MR and move on your next turn.

## Trading Properties

### How to Trade
1. Players can trade properties at any time
2. Both players must agree to the trade
3. Traded properties cannot be built on during current turn
4. You cannot trade mortgaged properties
5. The Banker monitors all trades

### Negotiation
- You can trade properties for other properties
- You can include cash in the trade
- Get Out of Jail Free cards can be traded

## Ending the Game

### Bankruptcy
A player is bankrupt when they cannot pay:
- Rent
- Taxes
- Card penalties

### Settlement Process
1. If you owe money but cannot pay, you must sell buildings and mortgage properties
2. Sell buildings to the Banker at half purchase price
3. Mortgage properties for half their value
4. If still unable to pay, you must declare bankruptcy

### Going Bankrupt
- All your properties pass to the player you owe money to
- If you owe the Banker, properties go back to the Banker
- Your token leaves the game
- Last player remaining wins the game

## Building in Sequence

### House Building Order
1. You cannot build more than one house ahead on any property in the group
2. You cannot build hotels until you have 4 houses on each property
3. All properties in a monopoly must have equal houses (within 1)
4. You cannot build during another player's turn

### Example
If you own all three light blue properties:
- Flora Fountain: must have the same number or one more house than Horniman Circle
- Horniman Circle: must have the same number or one more house than Apollo Bunder
- You cannot have 3 houses on Flora, 1 house on Horniman, and 0 on Apollo

## Special Rules

### Landing on Your Own Property
- You do not pay rent
- You can collect rent from others on your property

### Bankruptcy to Another Player
When a player owes you and declares bankruptcy:
1. Take all their properties
2. Collect their remaining cash
3. If any property has houses/hotels, you own those too
4. You now own that property completely

### Auctioning Unowned Properties
If you refuse to buy a property you landed on:
1. Banker puts it up for auction
2. Any player (including you) can bid
3. Highest bid wins
4. Winning bid is paid to the Banker

### Getting Out of Jail Early
You can leave Jail before your third turn by:
- Paying 50 MR (anytime)
- Rolling doubles (any turn)
- Playing a Get Out of Jail Free card

### Selling Buildings
1. You can only sell buildings to the Banker
2. You receive half the purchase price
3. You can only sell one house at a time
4. All properties in a group must be evenly developed (within 1 house)
5. You can sell during another player's turn if needed to pay debt

### Free Parking
- This space has no official rule in classic Monopoly
- Common house rule: Money accumulates in the center for landing on Free Parking
- In this version: Free Parking is simply a safe rest space

## Helpful Tips

### Strategy
1. Railroads and utilities provide steady income
2. Orange and red properties are landed on most frequently
3. Build houses on properties within a monopoly evenly
4. Don't spend all your cash on houses early in the game
5. Keep cash reserves for unexpected payments

### Quick Reference
- **Starting Money**: 1,500 MR
- **Go Salary**: 200 MR
- **Income Tax**: 200 MR or 10% of assets
- **Luxury Tax**: 100 MR
- **Jail Bail**: 50 MR
- **Property Groups**: 8 colors + 4 railways + 2 utilities
